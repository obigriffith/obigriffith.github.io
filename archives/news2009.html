16 Dec 2009 - No posts since July. That doesn't even seem possible. Funny to see such old news. Well, my trip to Berkeley went very well. In fact, that's where I'm heading in the New Year. I will be joining Joe Gray's lab at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory in Berkeley California. This is US DOE funded national lab located on the UC Berkeley campus. This is my last month at the Genome Sciences Centre in Vancouver. I posted some pictures to my flickr page from my trip to Berkeley and also camping on Vancouver Island with my siblings (3/4 of us - wish you were there too bro). Lately I'm listening to Black Lab and Echo and the Bunnymen. I'm going to be visiting friends and family during Christmas and then travelling for a while. So, don't expect regular posts. haha. Not that you would anyways.

23 July 2009 - Just got back from Saskatoon (flickr pics) and also got around to posting pictures from my trip to Boston. Next stop ... Berkeley California, the last destination on my postdoc job interview tour. I'm looking forward to that and also being done with the tour and hopefully settling on a new lab. Things are crazy at work - I've been continuing my analysis of Iressa response in cancer cell lines but also distracted by more statistical analysis of our anaplastic thryoid cancer tissue microarray data, adding several new datasets to ORegAnno, helping out with the identification of potential viral causes of lung cancer, and (news today) phase II of the big oral adenocarcinoma sequencing project. On top of all that, I'm moving at the end of the month to a new rental as the buyers take possession of our house. But, the weather has been great so all is well in the world.
What I'm listening to: Sigur Ros, Miles Davis, Gemma Hayes, The Prodigy (Invaders Must Die!), Beirut, Aimee Mann, Silversun Pickups, and Nada Surf.

13 July 2009 - Wow. So much to report. First, I sold my house. Its a relief to have that out of the way. I'm working on a new website summarizing results of my study of Iressa response study in various cancer cell lines (work in progress).

26 May 2009 - I've been busy getting my house ready to list on the market. Here is the result. I made this tour using TourVista. A very cool web service for making a virtual tour of your property.

30 Mar 2009 - Well. Where to begin. I have been busy as you may have guessed. Since writing last I have been to Florida for the AGBT conference. It was gorgeous, a lot of fun, and really interesting in terms of the science (flickr pics). The big next-gen sequencing companies (ABI, Illumina, Pacific Biosciences) really out-did themselves in terms of sponsorship and the conference organizers did a great job. After that, I went to New York (flickr pics) to interview for postdocs at Rockefeller, Weill-Cornell Medical, and Sloan-Kettering Memorial. All three seemed extremely promisiong and I would absolutely love to live in NY. But, I still want to see what the Boston and San Fran areas have to offer. In renovation land, we have finished building the new front porch and the backyard patio and are now ready for landscaping (weather permitting). Some plants are threatening to leaf out. But, it has been unseasonably cool. In terms of science work, things are progressing. I'm almost a tissue culture expert after the amount of time I've spent in the lab lately. I've tested dozens of potential cancer drugs in eight different cell lines. I also almost have all my whole transcriptome shotgun sequencing (WTSS, RNAseq) data and ChIPseq methylation data. So, I'm about ready to start writing up my paper on Iressa response and potential drug synergists in human cancer cell lines. I've also been helping my bro with some of his work on 5FU resistant colon cancer cell lines and am still collaborating with Dr. Wiseman on his thryoid cancer tissue microarrays. Lots to do!
What I'm listening to: new albums from TV on the Radio, U2, Black Lab, Lisa Hannigan, The Stills, Plain White T's, Beirut, and The Fray.
