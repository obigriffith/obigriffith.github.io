4 Dec 06 - Ouch. The whole month of November snuck by without a single update. I guess I've been busy. I was getting ready for the RegCreative Jamboree in Belgium and took off for some holidays in the Netherlands beforehand. I posted some pictures of Amsterdam and Ghent on my Flickr site. 23 October 06 - I finally added some new pictures to my flickr site. These were taken the hard way and developed manually in the darkroom. I forgot how much work traditional photography is. I got out the old pentax 35mm, a macro lens, and my tripod and headed down to Queen Elizabeth park. Took a bunch of closeups of flowers and such. Unfortunately, it was way too bright that day so a lot of my pictures were a little overexposed. I have a decent light meter, but on a day like that, it just says, "man its bright out here!". Getting back into the dark room for the first time in five years was like starting from scratch. Fun though. There are so many variables between taking the picture and the final product. You've got the aperture, shutter speed and focus when you take the picture; developing time, concentrations, and temperature while you're developing the negatives; then aperture, focus and filter grade on the enlarger; exposure time for the prints, and finally scanner settings. And of course, photoshop lets you change the digital image in a million ways. I bet there's also that many steps (or more) between light hitting an image and your brain processing it. Its a wonder that two people can ever agree on what they see given all the variations that likely exist in each of those processing steps. Anyways, after several hours of figuring out all of the above, here are my first attempts in the darkroom. My scanner didn't do a great job (making the images a little too dark) and I didn't yet avail myself of the aforemetioned photoshop features. But, you get the idea.
