2011 News (2010 | 2009 | 2008 | 2007 | 2006 | 2005)

14 Mar 2011 - Time for the now practically semi-annual update. Strangely as time goes by I seem to have less and less time for updates on the old personal blog. Even as we speak I should be doing about twenty other things. Ah, the joys of multi-tasking. :-) As always, Flickr provides a somewhat more current (although entirely pictoral) view of my recent doings. I can see from the last 2010 posting that I was just about to visit the Treasure Island Music Festival. Not a great venue but some really excellent music. Since then, the only shows I've been to were the entirely uninspiring Mix Master Mike and Donald Glover. But, on the other hand, I've been to some fun sporting events. I saw the Warriors play the Knicks and witnessed the historic collapse of Cal in the Big Game. I've continued to explore the bay area, visiting lots of great places like Muir Woods, Alcatraz Island, De Young Museum, Conservatory of Flowers, trippy fog-filled Page St, and the Mission District (new favorite SF hang out place). I recently got my motorcycle license and have been lucky enough to have my brother's bike (KLX400) for the last month. So, I've been taking rides out to places like Anthony Chabot Park and Orinda. I went down to San Diego for Thanksgiving to visit the gang down there, checked out the SD Zoo and also stopped by Hollywood on the way back. Conferences have recently taken me to Oregon, San Diego again, and Florida for this year's excellent AGBT conference. Most recently, Shirley was down for a reading week visit. We went on a nice motorcycle ride and checked out the surreal but extremely relaxing Esalen Institute. After listing all that, I can see why I've been too busy to post here. And, of course, I've said nothing of work. I got the review I mentioned previously completed and submitted. I'm continuing to process a large number of RNAseq datasets for different collaborators, still working on my meta-analysis of breast cancer relapse data and doing a very large integrative analysis to develop omic predictors of drug response in breast cancer. Upcoming meetings will take me back to Oregon and also to Boston in the summer. I have too much new music to listen to. But, at the moment I'm enjoying:
P.J. Harvey, Alexi Murdoch, and Mumford & Sons.
