15 Oct 2008 - Just got back from Thanksgiving long weekend in Nelson/Slocan. Now, I'm getting ready to give a guest lecture on the nucleus and chromatin tomorrow. Other than that, I'm just doing lots of analysis. I've also been doing some labwork for the first time in years. I have been helping my dad to develop a website for several properties in Honduras. Check it out at www.caribbeancottagecommunities.com. We have also made a Flickr page for this endeavour. We built a drainage ditch as part of our patio construction in the back yard. At work, I've also been busy looking for viral sequences in Illumina whole transcriptome sequence data and trying to identify new drugs to test for lymphoma. And, I'm trying to line up some teaching activities for this fall. Stay tuned for potentially exciting developments in that area. Its funny that in my last post I was complaining about getting that thyroid marker panel review off. Well, I did get it submitted and the editor sent it to four external reviewers! That's a lot of reviewers for a review article. I mean, all we're doing is summarizing the current literature. Maybe the editors don't want a Han-Warda controversy on their hands. Don't worry, we won't try to slip in anything about how thyroid tumors are really the result of an "Almighty Creator". Haha. So now I'm trying to get the revisions done and get it off again. It seems like I need to be procrastinating a really onerous task in order to post to my blog. :)

6 Jun 2008 - I'm busy finishing up a review of thyroid cancer biomarkers but I wanted to give you all a quick update. I received my diploma (pdf scan) today. Very cool. What a lot of trouble for that little piece of paper. haha. Too bad it doesn't say what its in (i.e., Medical Genetics). I've also updated my publications page with some recent developments. Our paper on the big tissue microrarray analysis of thyroid cancer was accepted for publication yesterday. My first 'last-author' paper! That's a big mile-stone and already makes my 'temporary' post-doc position here at the BCCRC seem a success. Before too long I'll have to start thinking about where I'm going next. But, for now, I'm trying to enjoy being a regular person in Vancouver. If only the weather would cooperate a little more. I'm also getting involved in some other interesting projects looking at pathogens in cancer using Illumina sequencing and doing some targetted drug discovery for follicular lymphoma. This fall I'd like to find some part-time teaching opportunities. On the fun side, I went to the Sasquatch music festival a couple of weekends ago. Pictures and videos should be available soon on my flickr page. There are some new pictures there taken by Chi on his recent trip to San Diego for Digestive Disease Week. Music I'm listening to these days: mostly stuff that I got hooked on at Sasquatch. The National were awesome and I also liked Michael Franti and Spearhead. That's all for now. Got to get this darn review off. :)

Everything went well and I'm really glad to be done. If you want to read my thesis you can find a pdf on the archive website. I have now started as a post-doc at the BC Cancer Research Centre while I figure out where to go next. I am finishing off some projects, starting some new Solexa and microarray analysis, and writing a review paper. That should keep me very busy for the next little while.I took a little time off to catch up on some yardwork (the fence is almost finished) and visit Slocan and Nelson. If the weather improves, I will soon be working on landscaping the backyard. My publications page has been updated.

16 Jan 2008 - Another year, another page for my blog. Its been a while since I've posted last and lots has happened. The number one, big thing, is that I finished writing my thesis and sent if off to my committee. Now, I just have to address any revisions they feel are necessary. And of course, lots of paperwork and scheduling to deal with. But, for the moment I am enjoying a brief respite in which I can catch up on some actual research and enjoy life a little more. Speaking of enjoying life, I have just returned from vacations to the interior (Nelson/Slocan) and the Big Island of Hawaii. Both were excellent. We had bad weather driving both ways on our trip to Nelson, but thanks to DS we now have excellent winter tires. That should make the drives up to Cypress, Whistler etc a lot less hairy. Hopefully it will also mean no more fun with the chains this season. Thanks man! There are some new winter shots of Chi and I cross-country skiing and moon-gazing in my Slocan valley set. And, of course, there are tons of pictures from Hawaii. The Hawaii set contains all 230 pics (filtered down from more than a thousand taken). But, if that's too overwhelming, you can also browse them by activity/location. Our home base was a nice 2-bedroom condo at the Paniolo Greens in Waikoloa village. This was up the hill from the coast about 10km, right on a golf course, with a conveniently located market, hot tub, pool, bbq, and so on. We started our first day with breakfast, drinks and sunbathing at the Hilton, one of the few places with a restaurant open on New Years day. Next, we went up the coast for snorkeling at Keauhou Bay and sightseeing at Pu'uhonua O Honaunau, the "place of refuge". A drive up Saddle Road with the top down and then some "offroading" took us to the breathtaking (and freezing cold) views and stargazing from the 14,000ft high Mauna Kea summit. A longer excursion to the eastern coast allowed us to explore the city of Hilo, Akaka falls, the Hawaii Tropical Botanical Gardens, and thermal pools near the deceased town of Kopoho (under a lava flow). Not satisfied with just easy-access snorkeling, we headed out in sea kayaks to visit the Captain Cook monument and excellent fish/coral viewing of Kealakekua Bay. What better way to relax from these busy day trips than the nearby Anaehoomalu Bay (or "A-bay" for the linguistically challenged) complete with gorgeous beach, traditional Hawaiian fish ponds, "jungle-beach-gym", and incredible sunsets. Our last big excursion was the requisite trip to Hawaii Volcanoes National Park for its volcanoes, lava tubes, craters, steam vents and more. The Thurston lava tube was especially cool. Thank goodness for headlamps. Finally, while waiting for our late night flight home we discovered the little gem that is Beach 69. It had it all. Twisted trees, killer surf, rainbows, and our last amazing Hawaiian sunset. You can see a whole collection of them in my Hawaii skies and sunsets set. Enjoy the pictures. I sure enjoyed taking and living them. :) More news to follow as I reluctantly come back to reality. Bye for now.
